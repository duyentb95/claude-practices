Retail Trader


# CONTEXT:

Exchanges/Brokers make their money from transaction fees, therefore their goal is to try to increase trading volumes on their own platforms. 

One way the platforms do this is by promoting whatever the “Hot Coin of the Day” is. 

When a coin is up a large amount in $\%$ , the exchange may do all sorts of things like promote the coin on social media or even list the coin at the very top of their exchange with flashy symbols and fire emojis to grab the attention of Retail Traders. 

This is because when Retail Traders see that a coin is up a lot of $\%$ , they can have thoughts like: 

- "everyone is making money in this coin except me!" 

- "this coin is up so much...what if it goes up even more!" 

They can experience a strong fear of missing out (FOMO). When Fear/Desire are amplified, it can increase the likelihood of a person taking action, in this case it will be to buy a coin in hopes of scoring some quick cash. 

# Why Platform Promotion is a good thing for a Momentum Regime:

When an exchange is heavily promoting a coin, it drives a lot of retail traders into that coin. 

When retail traders experience FOMO and enter a trade out of pure emotion, they will often do this just by slamming the MARKET BUY button without a plan and regardless of where price actually is on the chart. 

This means if we are already IN a trade, all the FOMO MARKET BUY entries from other retail traders actually help push the price in our favor.

# In Summary:

If a LARGE NUMBER of unsophisticated, emotionally-driven, unskilled players are randomly market buying AFTER our point of entry, this helps push the price from our entry towards our target.

# Bad Variable #3:

# Low Volatility / Sideways Price Action

# CONTEXT:

A big reason why the "grindy staircase" price action is so great for Momentum is because of it's high "margin of error". 

- the entry can be a bit earlier/later and you'll still win the trade. 

- the take profit can be a bit smaller/bigger and you'll still win the trade 

- the stoploss can be tighter/wider and you'll still win the trade. 

With a low volatility, choppy, sideways range this is the complete opposite. This is where Momentum Trades have the absolute LOWEST MARGIN OF ERROR and it can be incredibly difficult to win momentum trades (regardless of the strategy that is used).
