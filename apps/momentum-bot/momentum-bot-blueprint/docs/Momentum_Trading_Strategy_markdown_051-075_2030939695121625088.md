# Using LIMIT ORDERS in LOW VOLATILITY

If the distance from the entry level to the first relevant swing low is LESS THAN $3\%$ : 

- use a LIMIT ORDER to enter 

- this is because the impact from Market Order Fees will be very high when trying to trade smaller % moves. 

- After getting your entry trigger (1 candle close through the level), place the limit order directly on the resistance level that was just broken.

When thinking about the logic for exiting a trade, a sensible place to take profit would be at wherever the next Resistance Level is. 

This is because EVEN MORE PRESSURE (from market buys) is required to push the price beyond yet another level. 

Trading "level to level" keeps execution simple and results a lot more consistent compared to trying to go for a "moonshot" on every trade. 

It is important to understand that not every trade needs to be a home run. It's perfectly normal to go for 1R - 1.5R trades and allow the profits to stack up over time. 

# Some Nuances with Taking Profit

# All-Time High Breakouts

When trying to trade momentum at an All-Time High, there literally is no “next resistance level”. There is just empty space on the chart above. 

![image](https://cdn-mineru.openxlab.org.cn/result/2026-03-09/f88556de-5aa9-462a-958a-a1a8a9170b93/1b1cc06fbe8e32510a92d4184095d2f48a03064d5ae8157eacefcec900395e42.jpg)


There are 3 possible ways that I like to take profit when trading at an All-Time High 

1 (lowest risk, lowest payout): just play it safe and get out at +1R 

- No harm in banking a high probability 1R trade and moving onto the next one. 

#2 (medium risk, medium payout): exit at wherever the next big “sell wall” is. 

- Will need to look directly at the orderbook to be able to see where the next major level is. Sometimes it will be beyond the +1R point. 

3 (highest risk, highest payout): trailing the stoploss 

- When I have very high confidence in the trade (because of how the price action + volume look like together), I will consider trailing the stoploss upwards to whatever the "next swing low is". 

- That way if the coin really does end up "blasting off", as soon as it's done pumping it will break the structure to the downside and I will be forced to exit out of the trade.
